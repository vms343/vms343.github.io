About Manning

Manning Farnsworth Snyder is the Chief Operating Officer of NYU's Journal of Politics and International Affairs, an Editor on NYU's Undergraduate Law Review, and was a Guest Writer for the University of Chicago Law Review. For the past two summers, Manning was a Teaching Assistant (TA) at Columbia University, where he helped teach classes on "Human Rights" and "Legal Reasoning." He also has completed research for UNICEF's office at the United Nations, focusing on issues of world poverty and hunger. He works at Columbia University throughout the year as a "Proofreader" in their School of Professional Studies. Manning's work has been featured on CooperSquared.com and NYU's Journal of Politics and International Affairs.

Manning is a student at New York University, majoring in Journalism and Sociology. Originally from Charleston, South Carolina, Manning spent his freshman year of college in Paris, France at NYU Paris. During his time in Paris, Manning was the Editor-in-Chief of the NYU Paris Student Newspaper, where he was in charge of the day-to-day and publication responsibilities of the paper. He enjoys writing about politics, sports, and current events, as well as dabbling in speechwriting and copy editing. Manning is passionate about First Amendment law and the intersection between law and media, but is always looking to write on a wide-range of topics.
